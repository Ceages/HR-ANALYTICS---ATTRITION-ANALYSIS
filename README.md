## Analysis of Employee Attrition

The analysis examined factors influencing employee attrition within the company and revealed the following key findings:

1. **Salary Level and Attrition**

   * Employees in lower salary brackets exhibit a higher attrition rate compared to those in higher salary brackets.
   * This suggests that compensation may be a significant factor influencing employee retention.

2. **Departmental Attrition**

   * Employees in the Research and Development (R&D) department show a higher tendency to leave the company compared to employees in other departments.
   * This may indicate department-specific challenges that need further investigation.

3. **Educational Background**

   * Among employees' fields of education, those with a background in Life Sciences experience the highest attrition rate.
   * This trend suggests that employees from this field may have unique career expectations or opportunities outside the organization.

4. **Bonuses and Business Travel**

   * Employees who receive lower incentives or have limited business travel opportunities tend to have higher attrition rates.
   * This indicates that recognition, rewards, and professional exposure may influence employee satisfaction and retention.

5. **Job Roles**

   * Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives have the highest attrition rates among all job roles.
   * These positions may face specific challenges related to workload, career growth, compensation, or job satisfaction.

## Recommendations

Based on the findings, the following actions are recommended:

1. **Review Compensation Structure**

   * Reassess salary levels for employees in lower salary brackets to ensure competitiveness and fairness.
   * Consider periodic salary reviews and performance-based increments to improve retention.

2. **Address Attrition in the Sales Department**

   * Conduct a detailed assessment of employees in sales roles, particularly those with long tenure, to identify factors contributing to turnover.
   * Develop targeted retention strategies such as career advancement opportunities and performance incentives.

3. **Enhance Employee Incentives**

   * Provide additional bonuses, recognition programs, or alternative benefits for employees who have limited or no business travel opportunities.
   * Ensure reward systems are equitable and aligned with employee contributions.

4. **Focus on Life Sciences Employees**

   * Review compensation, benefits, and career development opportunities for employees with Life Sciences backgrounds.
   * Implement initiatives aimed at increasing engagement and job satisfaction within this group.

5. **Investigate High-Attrition Job Roles**

   * Conduct exit interviews and employee surveys for Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives to better understand the reasons behind their higher attrition rates.
   * Use the findings to improve working conditions, career development pathways, and welfare packages for these roles.

### Conclusion

Employee attrition appears to be strongly associated with compensation, department, educational background, incentives, and job role. By addressing these factors through targeted retention strategies, the company can improve employee satisfaction, reduce turnover, and enhance overall organizational performance.
